# PaymentMethod — Design Discussion & Decision

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Status:** Kept all 3 values — each is justified
**Affects:** `payments` table, `tenant_settings` (payment_cash, payment_aba_qr,
payment_card flags)
**MVP note:** CASH and ABA_QR are fully wired for MVP. CARD is a placeholder
enum value — no card payment integration exists yet.

---

## The enum

```sql
CREATE TYPE "PaymentMethod" AS ENUM (
  'CASH',
  'ABA_QR',
  'CARD'
);
```

---

## Part 1 — Each value explained in detail

### `CASH`

**Meaning:** The customer pays with physical currency (US dollars or
Cambodian riel) at the counter or to the server. There is no external
payment gateway — the merchant confirms receipt manually in the app.

**Who sets it:** System (when the customer selects "Cash" on the storefront
payment screen, or when the merchant creates a cash payment from the
merchant portal).

**What happens:**
- A `payments` row is created with method = CASH, status = PENDING.
- The storefront shows "Please pay at the counter"
  (សូមបង់ប្រាក់នៅបញ្ជរ).
- The merchant or kitchen staff sees a "Confirm cash received"
  (បានទទួលប្រាក់សុទ្ធ) button on the kitchen app or merchant portal.
- When the staff taps "Confirm," the payment moves to SUCCEEDED.
- No external API call, no gateway, no webhook.

**Real-world example:** At a rice plate stall (ហាងបាយ) in Phsar Toul Tom
Poung (Russian Market), a customer scans the QR code and orders chicken
rice with soup ($2.50). The stall uses PAY_BEFORE + CASH. The customer
walks to the counter and hands the stall owner 1 x $2 bill + 2000 riel
(~$0.50). The owner taps "Received cash" on their tablet. Payment →
SUCCEEDED. Kitchen ticket created. The cook starts preparing.

**Trust model:** Cash is entirely trust-based on the merchant side. There
is no system verification that money actually changed hands. The merchant's
tap is the truth. This is identical to how a traditional cash register
works — the cashier presses "Paid" and the drawer opens.

**Why it can't be removed:** Cash is king in Cambodia. While digital
payment adoption is growing rapidly (7x YoY for QR payments), cash remains
the most common payment method, especially for:
- Small stalls and food carts without smartphones for ABA
- Customers who don't have bank accounts (common in rural areas)
- Very small transactions ($0.50 - $1.00) where QR feels like overkill
- Tourists who haven't set up local mobile banking

A food ordering platform that doesn't support cash would exclude a
significant portion of the Cambodian market.

**Currency note:** Cambodia uses a dual-currency system:
- **USD (US Dollar):** Dominant for transactions above ~$1. Most food
  stalls price in USD. Change below $1 is given in riel.
- **KHR (Cambodian Riel):** Used for sub-dollar amounts. 1 USD ≈ 4,100 KHR.
  Coins don't circulate; smallest common unit is 100 riel notes.

The platform stores money in cents (USD) but merchants mentally operate in
this dual-currency system. A $2.50 order might be paid as $2 + 2000 riel.
The platform records it as $2.50 (250 cents) regardless of the physical
denomination mix.

### `ABA_QR`

**Meaning:** The customer pays by scanning a KHQR code with their mobile
banking app. The QR code is generated by the platform via ABA PayWay API.
Despite the name "ABA_QR," the generated KHQR code is scannable by any
of Cambodia's 30+ KHQR-participating banks — not just ABA.

**Who sets it:** System (when the customer selects "ABA QR" / "Pay with QR"
on the storefront payment screen).

**What happens:**
1. Platform calls ABA PayWay API to generate a KHQR code:
   - Uses the platform's own ABA PayWay merchant credentials
   - `payment_option: "abapay_khqr"` (NBC-standard, interoperable)
   - `payout` parameter routes funds to the merchant's personal ABA account
   - `tran_id` is platform-generated and stored as `payments.reference`
   - `callback_url` points to the platform's webhook endpoint
2. ABA returns a QR code string.
3. Storefront renders the QR code with a 5-minute countdown timer.
4. An "Open ABA app" deeplink button is shown for mobile users.
5. Customer scans with any KHQR-compatible banking app.
6. Customer confirms the pre-filled amount in their banking app.
7. Bank processes the payment.
8. ABA sends webhook to the platform's callback URL.
9. Platform verifies via ABA Check Transaction API (signed, outbound).
10. Payment → SUCCEEDED. Bill → PAID. Kitchen ticket created.

**Real-world example:** A tourist at a cafe (កាហ្វេ) in Pub Street, Siem
Reap, orders an iced coconut coffee ($3.00) and a mango shake ($3.50).
Total: $6.50. They select "Pay with QR" on the storefront. The storefront
displays a KHQR code. The tourist opens their Wing Bank app (not ABA — but
KHQR is interoperable), scans the code, sees "$6.50 to [merchant name],"
taps confirm. Wing processes the payment through the NBC clearing network.
ABA (the platform's acquiring bank) sends a webhook. Platform verifies.
Payment confirmed. Kitchen tablet beeps twice (two items on the ticket).

**Why it's called ABA_QR and not KHQR:** The enum value represents the
payment integration, not the QR standard. The platform integrates with ABA
PayWay as the payment gateway. ABA generates the KHQR code. The fact that
KHQR is interoperable is an implementation detail of ABA PayWay's
`abapay_khqr` payment option. If the platform later adds a second QR
gateway (e.g., Wing direct integration), it would be a new enum value
(`WING_QR`), not a modification of `ABA_QR`.

**Why it can't be removed:** ABA QR (via KHQR) is the most important
digital payment method in Cambodia:

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| ABA Mobile users | ~4.5 million |
| KHQR-participating banks | 30+ |
| QR payment growth (2023) | 7× YoY |
| Merchants accepting ABA/KHQR | 200,000+ |

A food ordering platform in Cambodia without KHQR support is solving half
the problem. Merchants already have personal ABA accounts and expect QR
payment capability. Customers expect to scan and pay. Omitting ABA QR
would be like launching a US food ordering platform without credit card
support.

**Platform aggregator model:** The platform holds a single ABA PayWay
merchant account. Individual merchants do NOT need their own ABA PayWay
registration. They only provide their personal ABA account number or
Bakong ID (phone number). The platform uses ABA's `payout` parameter to
route 100% of the payment to the merchant's account in real time. This is
critical because most Cambodian food merchants have never registered for
ABA PayWay — they just have a personal ABA bank account.

**Merchant onboarding for ABA QR:**
```
Owner enters: ABA account number (or phone number / Bakong ID)
              + account holder name
Platform validates format (no API call needed)
tenant_settings.payment_aba_qr = true
Done. Customers can now pay with QR.
```

**Security:** The platform's ABA API key is stored as an environment
variable, never in the database. HMAC-SHA512 (not SHA256) is used for
signing outbound API requests. Webhook verification uses the
check-transaction callback pattern, not HMAC on the inbound payload
(ABA does not sign outbound webhooks).

### `CARD`

**Meaning:** The customer pays with a credit or debit card. This is a
placeholder enum value for future use — no card payment integration exists
at MVP.

**Who sets it:** N/A at MVP. Future: system, when the customer selects card
payment on the storefront.

**What happens at MVP:**
- The enum value exists in the database and TypeScript contracts.
- `tenant_settings.payment_card` defaults to `false`.
- No storefront UI for card payment is shown.
- No gateway integration exists.
- If somehow a payment row is created with method = CARD, it would
  function like any other payment, but there's no gateway to process it.

**What will happen when wired (future):**
- Card payment UI on the storefront (card number input or tokenized
  payment form).
- Integration with a card payment gateway (candidates: ABA PayWay card
  API, Stripe, or a local acquirer).
- PCI DSS compliance requirements (tokenization, no raw card numbers in
  the platform's systems).
- Authorization + capture flow (PENDING → SUCCEEDED or FAILED).

**Real-world example (future):** A tourist at a restaurant doesn't have a
Cambodian banking app and doesn't carry cash. They select "Pay with card"
on the storefront, enter their Visa card number via a Stripe-style
tokenized form. The platform sends the token to the card gateway. Gateway
authorizes. Payment → SUCCEEDED.

**Why it's in the enum now even though it's not wired:**
1. **Schema stability:** Adding an enum value to PostgreSQL later requires
   `ALTER TYPE ... ADD VALUE`, which cannot be done inside a transaction
   and cannot be rolled back. Defining it now avoids a migration complexity
   later.
2. **TypeScript contract alignment:** `xfos/contracts/enums/index.ts`
   mirrors the Postgres enum. Having CARD in both places now means frontend
   and backend code can reference `PaymentMethod.CARD` for feature flags,
   conditional UI, and type checking — even before the integration is built.
3. **Tenant settings readiness:** `tenant_settings.payment_card` already
   exists as a boolean column (defaulting to `false`). When card payments
   are implemented, flipping this flag enables them per-tenant with no
   schema change.

**Why it can't be removed:** Removing it would mean adding it back later,
which is more disruptive than leaving a dormant value in the enum. The
cost of keeping it is zero — it occupies a few bytes in the enum type
definition and is never selected by any payment flow.

## Part 2 — State machine

PaymentMethod is not a state machine — it does not have transitions.
It's a classification enum: each payment row has exactly one method that
is set at creation and never changes. A payment that starts as ABA_QR
cannot become CASH.

However, the method determines which PaymentStatus transitions are valid:

### ABA_QR payment lifecycle

```
PENDING ──► SUCCEEDED   (webhook + check-transaction confirms)
   │
   ├──► FAILED          (gateway error)
   │
   └──► EXPIRED         (5-min QR timeout)
```

### CASH payment lifecycle

```
PENDING ──► SUCCEEDED   (merchant confirms receipt)
```

Cash payments do not fail or expire. If the customer doesn't pay cash,
the bill is voided — there's no FAILED cash payment.

### CARD payment lifecycle (future)

```
PENDING ──► SUCCEEDED   (authorization approved)
   │
   ├──► FAILED          (authorization declined)
   │
   └──► EXPIRED         (authorization timeout)
```

### Method availability per tenant

The available methods are controlled by boolean flags on `tenant_settings`:

```sql
payment_cash    BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT TRUE   -- always available by default
payment_aba_qr  BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE  -- requires ABA account setup
payment_card    BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE  -- not wired at MVP
```

The storefront's `GET /storefront/context/{token}` endpoint returns the
available payment methods dynamically:

```json
{
  "paymentMethods": ["CASH", "ABA_QR"]
}
```

A merchant who only accepts cash will have `["CASH"]`. A merchant with
ABA configured will have `["CASH", "ABA_QR"]`. The storefront UI renders
payment options based on this array.

## Part 3 — Cambodia payment context

### The dual-currency reality

Cambodia operates on a de facto dual-currency system:

| Currency | Usage | Common denominations |
|---|---|---|
| **USD** | Dominant for transactions ≥ $1. Prices at restaurants, cafes, and most food stalls are in USD. | $1, $5, $10, $20 bills. $50 and $100 exist but are avoided at small merchants (change is hard). |
| **KHR (Riel)** | Used for sub-dollar change. 1 USD ≈ 4,100 KHR. | 100, 500, 1000, 2000, 5000, 10000 riel notes. Coins do not circulate. |

**How this works in practice:** A customer orders food for $2.50. They hand
over $3. Change: 2,000 riel ($0.49). The merchant doesn't give $0.50 in
coins — they give the riel equivalent. The platform records the transaction
as $2.50 (250 cents) regardless of the physical currency mix.

### ABA PayWay and KHQR

**ABA PayWay** is the payment gateway operated by Advanced Bank of Asia
(ABA), Cambodia's largest bank by assets. It offers:

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| QR code generation | Dynamic KHQR codes with pre-filled amount |
| Payment options | `abapay` (ABA-only) and `abapay_khqr` (interoperable) |
| Webhook callbacks | POST to merchant callback URL on payment events |
| Check Transaction API | Server-to-server verification of payment status |
| Payout split | Route funds to third-party ABA accounts |
| Signing | HMAC-SHA512 for outbound requests |

**KHQR** (Khmer QR) is the national QR payment standard mandated by the
National Bank of Cambodia (NBC). It ensures interoperability — a KHQR code
generated by ABA can be scanned by any participating bank's mobile app:

| Participating banks (sample) | App |
|---|---|
| ABA (Advanced Bank of Asia) | ABA Mobile |
| Wing Bank | Wing Money |
| Canadia Bank | Canadia Mobile |
| ACLEDA Bank | ACLEDA Unity |
| Sathapana Bank | Sathapana Mobile |
| Bakong (NBC digital wallet) | Bakong |
| ... and 25+ more | ... |

**This is why the platform uses `abapay_khqr` (not `abapay`).** The
proprietary `abapay` option would only work for ABA customers. The KHQR
option works for customers of any Cambodian bank.

### Why the platform aggregator model

Most Cambodian food merchants (stalls, small restaurants) do not have ABA
PayWay merchant accounts. They have personal ABA bank accounts. The
traditional merchant QR code setup:

```
Merchant's printed static QR on the counter
    ↓
Customer scans → manually enters amount → pays
    ↓
Money lands in merchant's personal ABA account
    ↓
Platform: receives NOTHING. No webhook. No confirmation.
```

The platform aggregator model solves this:

```
Platform generates dynamic KHQR via its own ABA PayWay credentials
    ↓
Customer scans → amount pre-filled → pays
    ↓
ABA routes funds to merchant's ABA account (payout split, real-time)
    ↓
ABA sends webhook to PLATFORM's callback URL
    ↓
Platform verifies via check-transaction → SUCCEEDED
    ↓
Kitchen ticket fires automatically. No manual confirmation needed.
```

The merchant provides only their ABA account number or Bakong ID (phone
number). No PayWay registration. No API keys. No $100 setup fee.

## Part 4 — What's NOT in this enum (and why)

| Omitted value | What it would mean | Why we skip it |
|---|---|---|
| `WING_QR` | Direct integration with Wing Bank's QR API | Not needed. ABA's `abapay_khqr` generates KHQR codes that Wing users can already scan. A direct Wing integration would only be needed if Wing offered better rates or features, which is not the case at MVP. |
| `BAKONG` | Direct integration with NBC's Bakong digital wallet | Same as Wing — Bakong users can scan KHQR codes generated by ABA. No separate integration needed. |
| `BANK_TRANSFER` | Manual bank transfer (not QR) | Too slow for food ordering. Bank transfers take minutes to hours and have no real-time webhook. Not suitable for "order food → pay → eat" flow. |
| `E_WALLET` | Generic e-wallet (TrueMoney, Pi Pay, etc.) | Too vague. Each wallet would need its own integration and enum value. Pi Pay was acquired by ABA. TrueMoney (True Digital) has minimal Cambodia presence. Not worth the complexity at MVP. |
| `CRYPTO` | Cryptocurrency payment | Not relevant for Cambodian food stalls. |
| `BUY_NOW_PAY_LATER` | Deferred payment via fintech (Boost, etc.) | Not relevant for small food transactions ($1-$15). BNPL makes sense for $50+ purchases. |
| `APPLE_PAY` / `GOOGLE_PAY` | NFC/tokenized mobile payments | Very low adoption in Cambodia. Local banking apps dominate. Could be added if the platform expands to markets where these are common. |
| `PLATFORM_CREDIT` | Platform-issued credit or loyalty points | Not in MVP scope. Would require a credit/wallet system. Could be added as a payment method later if loyalty features are built. |

### Why not a reference table instead of an enum?

A `payment_methods` reference table would be more flexible — you could add
new methods without schema migration. But the trade-off isn't worth it:

1. **Payment methods require code changes.** Adding `WING_QR` means
   building a Wing API client, webhook handler, verification flow,
   storefront UI, receipt formatter. You can't "add a payment method"
   by inserting a database row — it requires engineering work.
2. **Type safety.** The enum is mirrored in TypeScript
   (`xfos/contracts/enums/index.ts`). The compiler catches invalid method
   references. A reference table would require runtime validation.
3. **Query performance.** `WHERE method = 'ABA_QR'` on an enum column is
   a simple equality check, not a join.
4. **Predictable set.** Cambodia has a small, stable set of payment rails.
   This isn't a marketplace with 50 payment methods — it's cash, QR, and
   eventually cards.

## Part 5 — Relationship to other enums and tables

### PaymentMethod → PaymentStatus (method-specific behavior)

| Method | Can be FAILED? | Can be EXPIRED? | Verification mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| `CASH` | No | No | Merchant attestation (tap "Received cash") |
| `ABA_QR` | Yes (gateway error) | Yes (5-min QR timeout) | ABA check-transaction API (signed, outbound) |
| `CARD` | Yes (auth declined) | Yes (auth timeout) | Gateway authorization response (future) |

### PaymentMethod → tenant_settings flags

| Enum value | tenant_settings column | Default | Enable condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| `CASH` | `payment_cash` | `true` | Always available. Merchant can disable if they only accept QR. |
| `ABA_QR` | `payment_aba_qr` | `false` | Merchant provides ABA account number/Bakong ID during onboarding |
| `CARD` | `payment_card` | `false` | Not wired at MVP. Future: merchant enables after card gateway setup |

### PaymentMethod → storefront context

The storefront fetches available payment methods from
`GET /storefront/context/{token}`:

```json
// Merchant with cash + ABA QR enabled:
{ "paymentMethods": ["CASH", "ABA_QR"] }

// Merchant with cash only:
{ "paymentMethods": ["CASH"] }

// Future merchant with all three:
{ "paymentMethods": ["CASH", "ABA_QR", "CARD"] }
```

The storefront dynamically renders payment method buttons based on this
array. If only CASH is available, the payment step shows a single "Pay at
counter" instruction instead of a method picker.

### PaymentMethod → receipt and bill display

The method affects how the receipt/bill summary is displayed:

| Method | Receipt shows | Bill detail |
|---|---|---|
| `CASH` | "Paid: Cash" / "បង់រួច: សាច់ប្រាក់" | No gateway reference |
| `ABA_QR` | "Paid: ABA QR" / "បង់រួច: ABA QR" + last 4 of payer account | Gateway reference (tran_id), approval code (apv) |
| `CARD` | "Paid: Card ****1234" (future) | Gateway reference, auth code |

## Part 6 — Decision summary

### Question: Why only 3 methods? Cambodia has many banking apps.

**Answer:** Cambodia has many banks, but they all speak KHQR. The platform
doesn't need a separate integration per bank. ABA PayWay generates KHQR
codes that are scannable by all 30+ participating banks. One integration
covers the entire Cambodian digital payment ecosystem. The three enum
values represent three distinct payment rails:
- CASH = physical currency, no technology
- ABA_QR = digital, QR-based, gateway-verified
- CARD = digital, card-based, gateway-verified (future)

### Question: Should we rename ABA_QR to KHQR?

**Answer: No.** The enum value represents the integration/gateway, not the
standard. The platform integrates with ABA PayWay specifically. If a second
QR gateway is added (unlikely but possible), it would be a new enum value,
not a modification of this one. ABA_QR accurately describes what the
platform calls: ABA's API, with ABA's credentials, verified via ABA's
check-transaction endpoint.

The customer-facing label can be "Pay with QR" (បង់ប្រាក់ជា QR) or
"KHQR" — that's a UI concern, not a schema concern.

### What we decided

- **Keep all 3 values.** CASH and ABA_QR are wired for MVP. CARD is
  reserved for future use.
- **ABA_QR uses `abapay_khqr` (KHQR), not `abapay` (ABA-proprietary).**
  KHQR is interoperable with all Cambodian banks. There is no reason to
  use the proprietary option.
- **Platform aggregator model for ABA QR.** One platform ABA PayWay
  account. Payout split routes funds to each merchant's personal ABA
  account. Merchants provide only their account number.
- **CARD is not wired at MVP.** It exists in the enum for schema stability
  and TypeScript type completeness. No card gateway integration will be
  built until post-MVP.
- **Enum over reference table.** Payment methods require code changes
  anyway. The enum provides type safety and keeps the allowed values
  explicit.
